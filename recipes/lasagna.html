How to make lasagna

Let's talk about bit first

Lasagna is a popular Italian dish made by layering wide, flat pasta noodles with meat or vegetable sauce, cheese, and sometimes other ingredients such as mushrooms or spinach. The dish is typically baked in the oven until the cheese on top is melted and bubbly.

Lasagna has a long history, dating back to ancient Greece and Rome, where a dish called "laganon" was made using layered pasta. However, the modern version of lasagna that we know today is thought to have originated in the city of Naples in Italy during the Middle Ages.
