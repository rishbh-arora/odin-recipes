How to make pasta

Let's talk about it first

Pasta is a staple food in Italian cuisine, and it has become a popular dish worldwide. It is made from a dough of durum wheat flour, eggs, and water, and then shaped into various forms such as spaghetti, fettuccine, penne, and many others.

Pasta can be cooked in many ways, such as boiled, baked, or fried, and served with various sauces, meats, and vegetables. It is a versatile food that can be easily incorporated into a wide variety of recipes, making it a go-to meal for many people.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Eggs
  • Salt
  • Water
  • sauces

Procedure:

  1. Add the flour and salt to a mixing bowl and whisk together.
  2. Create a well in the center of the flour mixture and crack the eggs into the well.
  3. Using a fork or your hands, gradually incorporate the flour into the eggs until a dough forms. If the dough is too dry, add water a teaspoon at a time until it comes together.
  4. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  6. After resting, dust a work surface with flour and roll out the dough using a rolling pin or pasta machine until it's the desired thickness.
  7. Cut the pasta into your desired shape (like fettuccine or spaghetti) using a pasta cutter or knife.
  8. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and add the pasta. Cook for 2-3 minutes (depending on the thickness of the pasta) until it's al dente.
  9. Drain the pasta and serve with your favorite sauce or toppings.
